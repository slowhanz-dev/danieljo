What makes us different

The way
we work.

Most designers hand off a drawing.
We hand off a product ready to make.

01

Material-first design

Every decision starts from an understanding of how porcelain actually behaves — not how it looks in a render. We design within the real constraints of the kiln, the glaze, and the hand.

02

Digital precision where it matters

We use Rhino 3D where exact dimensions are required — proportions, tolerances, stacking geometry. Digital tools are used because porcelain demands it, not as a stylistic choice.

03

Handcraft where the machine cannot

Some surfaces cannot be machined economically. A cylinder refined on a lathe costs a fraction of CNC at the same precision. We know where the hand wins, and we use it there.

04

Proportion obsession

A single handle is tested 20–30 times. Form dimensions are iterated at 0.5mm intervals. The difference between a cup you reach for and one you don't is almost never obvious — but it is always there.

05

Concept to production, in one studio

From brief to 3D model, prototype to ODM production — we manage the full cycle. No handoffs lost in translation. Over 500 café clients and international buyers in three continents.

06

Designed to be used, not admired

Beauty here is not decoration. It is the result of solving every problem correctly — ergonomics, stackability, glaze durability, cost of production. A well-made cup should feel inevitable.

Design in service
of material truth.

I make ceramic products where what is necessary is kept, and what is not is removed. Not from a philosophy of minimalism — but from a deep understanding of how porcelain actually behaves.

Digital tools are used where precision demands it. The hand is used where the material requires it. The result is always guided by one question: what is the most fitting form for this purpose?

Since 2020, I have operated Bunnar — a tableware brand supplying over 500 cafés across Korea, with buyers in the Netherlands, Australia, and Japan.
